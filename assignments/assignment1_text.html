Overview

This study benchmarks four distinct neural architectures for text classification using a filtered subset of the News Category Dataset. We evaluate traditional sequence models against modern Transformer-based approaches to identify the best trade-off between performance and training efficiency.

Architectures Compared

We explored a variety of architectures ranging from baseline sequence and local feature models (LSTM, CNN, RCNN) to modern pre-trained Transformers (DistilBERT, RoBERTa, ALBERT). Furthermore, we built ensemble pipelines combining predictions across models to capture complementary patterns.

04

Comparative Results

Extensive benchmarking confirms that pre-trained Transformers heavily outpace traditional models, with RoBERTa achieving an individual accuracy of 88.82%. Crucially, combining all models into a final ensemble strategy yielded the absolute highest results across the board—surpassing 90% accuracy and 86% Macro F1.
